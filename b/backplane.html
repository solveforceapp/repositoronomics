Backplane

Definition

A backplane is a circuit board containing sockets (slots) into which other circuit boards can be plugged. It serves as a backbone to connect several printed circuit boards together to make up a complete computer system or to expand its capabilities. Backplanes are commonly used in servers, routers, and industrial computers to provide a modular and scalable architecture.

Application

Backplanes are essential in systems that require high reliability and easy maintenance, such as telecommunications equipment, data centers, and industrial automation. They allow for hot-swapping of components, easy upgrades, and flexible system expansion without the need for extensive rewiring or downtime.
