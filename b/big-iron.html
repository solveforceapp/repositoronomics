Big Iron

Definition

Big Iron is a colloquial term for large, powerful mainframe computers used primarily by large organizations for bulk data processing, critical applications, and transaction processing. These systems are known for their reliability, scalability, and high throughput.

Application

Big Iron systems are used in banking, government, and enterprise environments where uptime, security, and the ability to handle massive workloads are essential. They support core business operations, such as transaction processing and large-scale database management.
