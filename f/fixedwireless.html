Fixed Wireless

Definition

Fixed wireless is a type of broadband internet access that uses radio signals to connect two fixed locations, such as a home or business and a service providerâ€™s tower. It eliminates the need for physical cables, offering high-speed connectivity in areas where wired infrastructure is impractical.

Application

Fixed wireless is used to deliver internet service in rural or underserved regions, provide backup connectivity, and support temporary installations. For example, a business in a remote area may use fixed wireless to access high-speed internet without waiting for fiber deployment.
