Data Center

Definition

A Data Center is a facility used to house computer systems and associated components, such as telecommunications and storage systems. It provides critical infrastructure for processing, storing, and managing large volumes of data.

Application

Data centers are essential for cloud computing, enterprise IT, and telecom networks. They support business operations, disaster recovery, and digital transformation by ensuring high availability, security, and scalability of IT resources.
