Bottleneck

Definition

A bottleneck is a point of congestion in a system where the performance or capacity is limited by a single component. It restricts the overall throughput, causing delays and reducing efficiency in hardware, software, or processes.

Application

Bottlenecks are identified and addressed in IT, manufacturing, and business operations to optimize performance. In computing, upgrading the slowest component (e.g., CPU, memory, or network) can eliminate bottlenecks and improve system speed.
