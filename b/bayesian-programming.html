Bayesian Programming

Definition

Bayesian programming is a formalism and methodology for specifying probabilistic models and solving problems when information is incomplete or uncertain. It uses Bayesian inference to update beliefs based on observed data, enabling robust decision-making in complex environments.

Application

Bayesian programming is used in artificial intelligence, robotics, and data science for tasks such as prediction, classification, and sensor fusion. It allows systems to reason under uncertainty and adapt to new information, improving accuracy and reliability.
