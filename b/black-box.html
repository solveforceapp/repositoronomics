Black Box

Definition

A black box is a system, device, or component whose internal workings are not known or accessible to the user. Only the inputs and outputs are visible, making it useful for testing and abstraction in hardware and software engineering.

Application

Black box testing is used in software quality assurance to validate functionality without knowledge of internal code. In hardware, black boxes are used to encapsulate proprietary technology or simplify complex systems for integration and troubleshooting.
