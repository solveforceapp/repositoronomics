Fax over IP

Definition

Fax over IP (FoIP) is a technology that enables the transmission of fax documents over Internet Protocol (IP) networks, such as the internet or private IP-based networks. It replaces traditional analog phone lines with digital communication, using protocols like T.38 or G.711 to ensure reliable fax delivery.

Application

FoIP is used by businesses to integrate faxing into their unified communications systems, reduce costs, and improve efficiency. For example, a company can send and receive faxes directly from email or document management systems, eliminating the need for physical fax machines and dedicated phone lines.
