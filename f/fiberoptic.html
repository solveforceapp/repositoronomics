Fiber Optic

Definition

Fiber optic refers to the technology that transmits data as pulses of light through strands of glass or plastic fibers. It offers extremely high bandwidth and low signal loss over long distances, making it ideal for telecommunications, internet backbones, and high-speed data networks.

Application

Fiber optic cables are used by ISPs, telecom carriers, and enterprises to deliver high-speed internet, connect data centers, and support cloud services. For example, metropolitan area networks (MANs) and submarine cables rely on fiber optics for reliable, high-capacity communication.
