Byzantine

Definition

Byzantine refers to a class of failures in distributed systems where components may fail and provide conflicting or malicious information to different parts of the system. The term originates from the Byzantine Generals Problem, a classic issue in computer science.

Application

Byzantine fault tolerance is critical in blockchain, distributed databases, and secure communications. Algorithms designed to handle Byzantine faults ensure system reliability and consensus even when some nodes act unpredictably or maliciously.
