Big Data

Definition

Big Data refers to extremely large and complex data sets that are difficult to process and analyze using traditional data management tools. It encompasses the volume, velocity, and variety of data generated from various sources, including social media, sensors, and transactions.

Application

Big Data is used in business intelligence, healthcare, finance, and scientific research to uncover patterns, trends, and insights. Advanced analytics and machine learning techniques are applied to Big Data to drive decision-making, optimize operations, and create new products and services.
