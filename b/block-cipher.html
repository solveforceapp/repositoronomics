Block Cipher

Definition

A block cipher is a symmetric key encryption algorithm that encrypts data in fixed-size blocks, typically 64 or 128 bits, rather than one bit at a time. It is widely used for securing data in transit and at rest.

Application

Block ciphers are used in protocols like SSL/TLS, disk encryption, and secure communications. Examples include AES and DES, which protect sensitive information in banking, government, and enterprise systems.
