Deep Packet Inspection

Definition

Deep Packet Inspection (DPI) is a network filtering technique that examines the data part (and possibly the header) of a packet as it passes an inspection point, enabling advanced traffic management and security.

Application

DPI is used by ISPs, enterprises, and security providers to detect threats, enforce policies, and optimize network performance.
