Coaxial Cable

Definition

Coaxial cable is a type of electrical cable with an inner conductor, insulating layer, metallic shield, and outer cover, used to transmit high-frequency signals with low loss.

Application

Coaxial cables are widely used for cable television, internet, and telecommunications, providing reliable signal transmission for broadband and RF applications.
