ADSL (Asymmetric Digital Subscriber Line)

Definition

ADSL is a type of digital subscriber line (DSL) technology that enables faster data transmission over copper telephone lines than a conventional voiceband modem can provide. It is called "asymmetric" because the download and upload speeds are not equal, with download speeds typically much higher.

Application

ADSL is widely used for residential and small business internet access, providing broadband connectivity for web browsing, streaming, and VoIP. It allows simultaneous voice and data transmission over the same line, making it cost-effective for consumers.
