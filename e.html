E Terms

E-Commerce

E-commerce, or electronic commerce, refers to the buying and selling of goods and services over the internet. It encompasses online retail, digital marketplaces, business-to-business (B2B) transactions, and mobile commerce. E-commerce platforms provide secure payment processing, inventory management, and customer engagement tools, revolutionizing global trade and consumer behavior.

E-Learning

E-learning is the use of digital technologies to deliver educational content and facilitate learning outside traditional classrooms. It includes online courses, virtual classrooms, webinars, and interactive modules, enabling flexible, self-paced, and remote education for individuals and organizations.

E-Mail

Email (electronic mail) is a method of exchanging digital messages between users over computer networks. Email systems support attachments, encryption, filtering, and integration with productivity tools, making them essential for business and personal communication.

E-SIM

An eSIM (embedded SIM) is a programmable SIM card integrated into mobile devices, allowing users to switch carriers and manage profiles without physical SIM cards. eSIM technology simplifies device activation, supports multiple profiles, and enhances IoT connectivity.

E2EE (End-to-End Encryption)

End-to-end encryption (E2EE) is a security mechanism that ensures data is encrypted on the sender’s device and only decrypted by the intended recipient. E2EE protects messages, files, and calls from interception, providing privacy in messaging apps, email, and cloud storage.

Edge Computing

Edge computing is a distributed computing paradigm that processes data near the source of generation, reducing latency and bandwidth usage. Edge devices, such as IoT sensors and gateways, enable real-time analytics and decision-making in smart cities, manufacturing, and autonomous vehicles.

EEPROM

Electrically Erasable Programmable Read-Only Memory (EEPROM) is a type of non-volatile memory that can be electrically erased and reprogrammed. EEPROM is used in firmware storage, embedded systems, and configuration data retention.

Elasticity

Elasticity in cloud computing refers to the ability to dynamically allocate and release resources based on demand. Elastic systems automatically scale up or down, optimizing performance and cost for applications and services.

Electromagnetic Interference (EMI)

Electromagnetic interference (EMI) is unwanted disturbance caused by electromagnetic fields affecting electronic devices. EMI can degrade performance, cause data loss, or disrupt communication. Shielding, grounding, and filtering are used to mitigate EMI in hardware design.

Electronic Data Interchange (EDI)

Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) is the automated exchange of business documents between organizations in a standardized electronic format. EDI streamlines supply chain operations, reduces errors, and accelerates transaction processing in industries like retail, logistics, and healthcare.

Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT)

Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT) is the movement of money between bank accounts using electronic systems. EFT includes direct deposit, wire transfers, and online payments, providing secure, efficient, and paperless financial transactions.

Electronic Health Record (EHR)

An Electronic Health Record (EHR) is a digital version of a patient’s medical history, maintained by healthcare providers. EHRs improve care coordination, data accuracy, and patient outcomes through secure, real-time access to health information.

Electronic Signature (E-Signature)

An electronic signature is a digital mark or authentication used to sign documents electronically. E-signatures are legally recognized in many jurisdictions and streamline contract management, approvals, and workflow automation.

Electrostatic Discharge (ESD)

Electrostatic discharge (ESD) is the sudden flow of electricity between two objects caused by contact or a short circuit. ESD can damage sensitive electronic components, so precautions like grounding and antistatic materials are used in manufacturing and repair.

Elevated Privileges

Elevated privileges refer to higher levels of access or permissions granted to users or processes, allowing them to perform administrative tasks. Managing elevated privileges is critical for security and compliance in IT systems.

Emulation

Emulation is the process of mimicking the behavior of one system using another, often through software. Emulators enable legacy software to run on modern hardware, support cross-platform development, and facilitate testing and debugging.

Encryption

Encryption is the process of converting data into a coded format to prevent unauthorized access. Encryption algorithms, such as AES and RSA, are fundamental to cybersecurity, protecting data at rest and in transit.

Endpoint

An endpoint is any device or node that communicates on a network, such as computers, smartphones, servers, or IoT devices. Endpoint security focuses on protecting these devices from threats and unauthorized access.

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) is integrated software that manages business processes, including finance, HR, supply chain, and manufacturing. ERP systems centralize data, improve efficiency, and support decision-making in organizations.

EPROM

Erasable Programmable Read-Only Memory (EPROM) is a type of non-volatile memory that can be erased by exposure to ultraviolet light and reprogrammed. EPROM is used in embedded systems and firmware storage.

Error Correction Code (ECC)

Error Correction Code (ECC) is a method of detecting and correcting data errors in memory, storage, and communication systems. ECC improves data integrity and reliability, especially in mission-critical applications.

Ethernet

Ethernet is a family of networking technologies used for local area networks (LANs). It defines standards for cabling, signaling, and data transmission, enabling high-speed, reliable communication between computers and devices.

Exabyte

An exabyte is a unit of digital information equal to 1,024 petabytes or approximately one billion gigabytes. Exabytes are used to measure massive data volumes in cloud storage, big data analytics, and internet traffic.

Executable File

An executable file is a program file that can be run by a computer’s operating system. Executable files contain machine code instructions and have extensions like .exe (Windows), .app (macOS), or .out (Linux).

Expansion Card

An expansion card is a hardware component that adds functionality to a computer, such as graphics, sound, or network capabilities. Expansion cards are installed in motherboard slots and enhance system performance and features.

Expansion Slot

An expansion slot is a connector on a computer motherboard that allows the installation of expansion cards. Common slot types include PCI, PCIe, and AGP, supporting hardware upgrades and customization.

Expert System

An expert system is an artificial intelligence program that simulates the decision-making ability of a human expert. Expert systems use knowledge bases and inference engines to solve complex problems in fields like medicine, finance, and engineering.

Exploit

An exploit is a technique or software that takes advantage of vulnerabilities in systems or applications to gain unauthorized access or cause harm. Exploit prevention is a key aspect of cybersecurity defense.

Exposure

Exposure in cybersecurity refers to the state of being vulnerable to threats or attacks due to weaknesses in systems, processes, or configurations. Risk assessments identify and mitigate exposures to protect assets.

Extended Reality (XR)

Extended Reality (XR) is an umbrella term for immersive technologies, including virtual reality (VR), augmented reality (AR), and mixed reality (MR). XR applications enhance training, entertainment, and collaboration experiences.

External Hard Drive

An external hard drive is a portable storage device that connects to computers via USB, Thunderbolt, or eSATA. External drives provide additional storage, backup, and data transfer capabilities for users and organizations.

Extranet

An extranet is a private network that grants controlled access to external partners, vendors, or customers. Extranets facilitate collaboration, data sharing, and secure communication beyond organizational boundaries.

Eye Tracking

Eye tracking is a technology that measures eye movement and gaze direction, used in user experience research, assistive devices, and gaming. Eye tracking enables hands-free interaction and accessibility for users with disabilities.

Ethernet Switch

An Ethernet switch is a network device that connects multiple devices within a LAN, forwarding data packets based on MAC addresses. Switches improve network efficiency, reduce collisions, and support VLAN segmentation.

Event-Driven Architecture

Event-driven architecture is a software design pattern where system components communicate by producing and consuming events. This approach enables scalable, loosely coupled, and responsive applications in cloud and microservices environments.

Exception Handling

Exception handling is a programming technique for managing errors and unexpected conditions during program execution. Proper exception handling improves software reliability, user experience, and maintainability.

Ethernet Cable

An Ethernet cable is a physical cable used to connect devices in a wired network. Common types include Cat5e, Cat6, and Cat7, supporting various speeds and distances for data transmission.

EEPROM Programmer

An EEPROM programmer is a device used to write data to EEPROM chips, enabling firmware updates, device configuration, and embedded system development.

Edge Device

An edge device is a networked hardware component that processes data at the edge of a network, close to the data source. Edge devices include routers, gateways, and IoT sensors, supporting real-time analytics and decentralized computing.

Elastic Load Balancer (ELB)

An Elastic Load Balancer (ELB) is a cloud service that distributes incoming network traffic across multiple servers, improving application availability, scalability, and fault tolerance.

Embedded System

An embedded system is a dedicated computer system designed to perform specific functions within a larger device. Embedded systems are found in appliances, vehicles, medical devices, and industrial equipment.

End User

An end user is the individual who ultimately uses a product, service, or system. Understanding end user needs is critical for designing effective, user-friendly solutions in technology and business.

Enterprise Network

An enterprise network is a large-scale, private network that connects computers, devices, and applications within an organization. Enterprise networks support secure communication, resource sharing, and centralized management.

Ethernet Adapter

An Ethernet adapter is a hardware component that enables devices to connect to wired Ethernet networks. Adapters can be integrated into motherboards or provided as expansion cards or USB dongles.

Event Log

An event log is a record of system, application, or security events generated by operating systems and software. Event logs are used for troubleshooting, auditing, and compliance monitoring.

Exfiltration

Exfiltration is the unauthorized transfer of data from a system or network, often as part of a cyberattack. Data loss prevention (DLP) tools and monitoring help detect and prevent exfiltration incidents.

Exponential Backoff

Exponential backoff is a network protocol technique that increases the wait time between retries after each failure, reducing congestion and improving reliability in distributed systems.

External Storage

External storage refers to devices or media used to store data outside a computer’s internal storage, such as USB drives, SD cards, and network-attached storage (NAS). External storage provides portability, backup, and data sharing capabilities.

Eye Strain

Eye strain is discomfort or fatigue caused by prolonged use of digital screens. Ergonomic practices, screen filters, and regular breaks help reduce eye strain for computer users.
