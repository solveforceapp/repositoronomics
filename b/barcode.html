Barcode

Definition

A barcode is a machine-readable code in the form of numbers and a pattern of parallel lines of varying widths, used for identifying products, tracking inventory, and automating data entry in retail, logistics, and manufacturing.

Application

Barcodes are widely used in supply chain management, retail checkout systems, and asset tracking. They enable fast, accurate data capture and reduce human error in inventory and sales processes.
