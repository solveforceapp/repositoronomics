Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)

Definition

Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) is a policy that allows employees to use their personal devices, such as smartphones, tablets, and laptops, for work purposes. BYOD increases flexibility and mobility but introduces security and management challenges.

Application

Organizations implement BYOD policies to reduce hardware costs and support remote work. Security measures like mobile device management (MDM) and endpoint protection are essential to safeguard corporate data on personal devices.
