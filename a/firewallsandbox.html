Firewall Sandbox

Definition

A firewall sandbox is a security feature that isolates and analyzes potentially malicious files or traffic in a controlled environment before allowing them into the main network. This helps prevent malware, zero-day attacks, and other threats from compromising critical systems.

Application

Firewall sandboxes are used in advanced threat protection solutions for enterprises and service providers. For example, an email security gateway may use a sandbox to open attachments and detect hidden malware, blocking threats before they reach end users.
