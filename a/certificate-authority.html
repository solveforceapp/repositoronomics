Certificate Authority

Definition

A Certificate Authority (CA) is a trusted organization that issues digital certificates used to verify the identity of websites, individuals, and devices, enabling secure encrypted communications over networks.

Application

Certificate Authorities are essential for SSL/TLS, VPNs, and secure email, providing the foundation for trust in e-commerce, online banking, and secure enterprise communications.
