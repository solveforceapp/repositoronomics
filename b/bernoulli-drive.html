Bernoulli Drive

Definition

A Bernoulli Drive is a high-capacity removable floppy disk drive developed by Iomega in the 1980s. It used the Bernoulli principle to keep the disk from touching the read/write head, reducing wear and increasing reliability compared to traditional floppy drives.

Application

Bernoulli Drives were used for data backup and transfer in personal computers and workstations before the advent of more modern storage solutions. They provided a reliable, portable option for large data storage in their era.
