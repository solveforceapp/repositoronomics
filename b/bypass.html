Bypass

Definition

Bypass refers to the act of circumventing a system, device, or security control, often to maintain functionality or gain unauthorized access. In networking, it can mean routing traffic around a failed or restricted component.

Application

Bypass techniques are used in network design for redundancy and fault tolerance, ensuring continuous service during failures. In cybersecurity, bypassing security controls is a common attack method, making robust defense mechanisms essential to prevent unauthorized access.
