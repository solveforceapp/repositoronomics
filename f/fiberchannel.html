Fiber Channel

Definition

Fiber Channel is a high-speed network technology primarily used to connect computer data storage to servers in storage area networks (SANs). It provides fast, reliable, and lossless data transfer, supporting speeds from 1 Gbps to 128 Gbps and beyond.

Application

Fiber Channel is essential in enterprise data centers for connecting servers to storage arrays, enabling high-performance applications such as databases, virtualization, and backup systems. Its low latency and high reliability make it a preferred choice for mission-critical workloads.
