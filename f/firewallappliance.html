Firewall Appliance

Definition

A firewall appliance is a dedicated hardware device designed to protect networks by monitoring and controlling incoming and outgoing traffic based on predetermined security rules. Unlike software firewalls, appliances are purpose-built for high performance and reliability, often including advanced features like intrusion prevention, VPN support, and content filtering.

Application

Firewall appliances are deployed at network perimeters in businesses, data centers, and ISPs to block unauthorized access and cyber threats. For example, a company may use a firewall appliance to segment its internal network from the internet, ensuring only legitimate traffic is allowed and sensitive data is protected.
