Antivirus

Definition

Antivirus software is a program designed to detect, prevent, and remove malicious software (malware) such as viruses, worms, and trojans from computers and networks. It uses signature-based detection, heuristics, and real-time monitoring to protect systems.

Application

Antivirus solutions are essential for individuals and organizations to safeguard data, maintain system integrity, and prevent cyberattacks. They are deployed on endpoints, servers, and gateways, often as part of a broader cybersecurity strategy.
