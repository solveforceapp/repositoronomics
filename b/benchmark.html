Benchmark

Definition

A benchmark is a standard test or set of tests used to measure the performance of hardware, software, or systems. It provides objective data for comparing different products or configurations under controlled conditions.

Application

Benchmarks are used in IT, engineering, and research to evaluate CPUs, storage devices, networks, and applications. They help guide purchasing decisions, optimize system performance, and validate improvements after upgrades or changes.
