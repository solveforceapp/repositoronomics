Bridge

Definition

A bridge is a networking device that connects and filters traffic between two or more network segments, operating at the data link layer (Layer 2) of the OSI model. It helps manage traffic, reduce collisions, and extend network size.

Application

Bridges are used in LANs to segment networks, improve performance, and isolate traffic. In hardware, bridges connect different bus architectures, such as PCI to ISA, enabling communication between components with different protocols.
