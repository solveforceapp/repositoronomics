Access Point

Definition

An Access Point (AP) is a networking hardware device that allows Wi-Fi devices to connect to a wired network. It acts as a central transmitter and receiver of wireless radio signals, enabling wireless communication within a local area network (LAN).

Application

Access Points are commonly used in homes, offices, and public spaces to provide wireless internet access. In enterprise environments, multiple APs are deployed to ensure seamless coverage and support for a large number of users and devices. They are essential for enabling mobility, BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) policies, and IoT connectivity.
