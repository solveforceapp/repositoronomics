Bit Rate

Definition

Bit rate, or bitrate, is the number of bits transmitted or processed per unit of time in telecommunications and computing. It is typically measured in bits per second (bps) and determines the speed and quality of data transmission.

Application

Bit rate is a key metric in digital communications, affecting the quality of audio, video, and network connections. Higher bit rates enable better quality and faster data transfer, while lower bit rates are used for bandwidth efficiency in constrained environments.
