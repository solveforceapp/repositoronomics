Cable Modem

Definition

A cable modem is a network device that enables high-speed internet access by connecting a computer or router to a cable television line. It modulates and demodulates data signals over coaxial cable infrastructure, providing broadband connectivity for residential and business users.

Application

Cable modems are widely used for home and small business internet access, supporting activities such as streaming, online gaming, VoIP, and remote work. They are essential for ISPs delivering broadband services over existing cable TV networks, offering scalable and reliable connectivity.
