Accounting

Definition

In telecom and IT, Accounting refers to the process of tracking the usage of network resources by users or devices. It involves collecting, measuring, and reporting data on resource consumption for purposes such as billing, auditing, and capacity planning.

Application

Accounting systems are used by service providers to generate invoices for customers based on their usage of services like bandwidth, storage, or call minutes. In enterprise networks, accounting helps monitor resource allocation, detect abuse, and optimize costs.
