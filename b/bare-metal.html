Bare Metal

Definition

Bare metal refers to computer hardware that is used without any operating system or virtualization layer. It allows direct access to the physical resources, providing maximum performance and control for specialized applications.

Application

Bare metal servers are used in environments where performance, security, and customization are critical, such as high-performance computing, scientific research, and enterprise data centers. They are ideal for workloads that require dedicated resources and minimal overhead.
