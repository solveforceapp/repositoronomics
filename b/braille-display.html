Braille Display

Definition

A Braille display is an assistive device that enables visually impaired users to read digital text by converting it into tactile Braille characters. It uses a series of pins that move up and down to form Braille cells, providing real-time access to computer content.

Application

Braille displays are used in education, employment, and daily life to provide accessibility to computers, smartphones, and other digital devices. They empower users to read, write, and interact with technology independently.
