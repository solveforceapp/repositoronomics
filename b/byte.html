Byte

Definition

A Byte is a unit of digital information that consists of eight bits. It is the basic addressable element in many computer architectures and is used to represent a single character of data, such as a letter or number.

Application

Bytes are fundamental in computing, networking, and data storage. File sizes, memory, and bandwidth are commonly measured in bytes and their multiples (KB, MB, GB, etc.).
