Bioinformatics

Definition

Bioinformatics is an interdisciplinary field that combines biology, computer science, mathematics, and statistics to analyze and interpret biological data. It involves the development of algorithms, databases, and tools for managing and understanding complex biological information.

Application

Bioinformatics is essential in genomics, drug discovery, and personalized medicine. It enables researchers to analyze DNA sequences, predict protein structures, and identify genetic variations, driving advances in healthcare and biotechnology.
