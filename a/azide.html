Azide

Azide refers to the anion N3âˆ’ or compounds containing this group. Azides are highly reactive and are used in a variety of chemical, biological, and medical applications. In biotechnology and neurotechnology, azides are commonly used in click chemistry, a technique for labeling and modifying biomolecules, and as inhibitors of cellular respiration in research settings.

Application

In biotechnology, azides are used for bioconjugation, drug development, and the synthesis of complex molecules. Click chemistry involving azides enables the efficient and selective labeling of proteins, nucleic acids, and other biomolecules for imaging, diagnostics, and therapeutic applications. In neurotechnology, azides are used to study mitochondrial function, inhibit specific enzymes, and develop targeted delivery systems. Safety precautions are essential when handling azides due to their potential toxicity and explosiveness. Advances in chemical biology, materials science, and synthetic chemistry continue to expand the utility of azides in research and medicine.
