Cybersquatting

Definition

Cybersquatting is the practice of registering, trafficking in, or using an Internet domain name with the intent to profit from the goodwill of a trademark belonging to someone else. It often involves purchasing domain names similar to established brands or businesses and attempting to sell them at a premium.

Application

Cybersquatting is addressed by laws and regulations such as the Anti-Cybersquatting Consumer Protection Act (ACPA). Organizations monitor and protect their domain portfolios to prevent brand abuse and legal disputes.
